The five things no YouTube guide tells you

These are the exact walls people hit doing this conversion — each one is covered in depth in the wiring package.

1️⃣

There is no "upload" step

Forget Arduino IDE and forget clicking Upload in VS Code. This board flashes itself: put firmware.bin on the microSD card, power on, wait ~30 seconds. The file renaming itself to FIRMWARE.CUR is your proof it worked.

2️⃣

Anet endstops have one wire too many

Anet's 3-wire endstop modules meet the SKR's 2-pin ports. Only the two switch wires connect — the middle 5 V wire is for the module's LED and must be insulated. Beep-test the pair, then check with M119 before you ever home.

3️⃣

The "dead" fans are on purpose

FAN0 (part cooling) is off until the slicer or M106 commands it. FAN1 only spins while motors are enabled. The hotend heatsink fan gets hardwired to the PSU, always on — that's factory practice on Ender 3s, and skipping it causes heat-creep jams.

4️⃣

The fan ports output 24 V

Fan headers pass PSU voltage straight through. On a 24 V machine like the A8 Plus, a 12 V fan on those ports burns out. Keep 12 V fans and LED strips on a separate 24→12 V module.

5️⃣

The TFT35 E3 is two screens in one

RS232 cable to the TFT port = touchscreen mode (115200 baud). EXP3 ribbon to EXP1 = classic Marlin knob mode. Wire both, hold the knob ~2 seconds to switch. This firmware supports both out of the box.
