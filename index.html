THE INDUSTRIAL SIDE

We get inside the machines that run a plant floor.

A software studio first — and quietly, an industrial superpower. The hardware that keeps a plant running rarely gives up its data easily. We reverse-engineer the protocol, pull the real numbers off the controller, and put them on a screen an operator actually wants to look at. Live dashboards, shift-cost reporting, machine troubleshooters, and ERP hand-off — polished like everything else we ship.

Speaks the floor's protocolsModbus, OPC UA, MQTT, serial/RS-485, plus Allen-Bradley & Siemens tooling — even proprietary buses like Maguire's MLAN.
Maguire blender fleets, instrumentedLive dashboards, per-shift cost analytics, batch-drop logging, and clean hand-off into eIQ / DELMIAWorks.
Troubleshooters for real machinesFault libraries, live PLC monitors, I/O maps and searchable ladder knowledge — built private, per plant.
Engineering deliverables, not just appsJIC electrical prints, PLC code recovery, and an AI search engine across ~1,000 plant manuals.

PHILOSOPHY

Why HONEYBADGER

The honey badger doesn't care. It chews through firmware bugs vendors won't fix, walks past "this can't be done" forum threads, and ships the polished thing anyway.
  • One-click installers, no terminals. A user shouldn't need to know what Python is to use the software.
  • Honest about limits. Every product ships with a Known Issues page on day one.
  • No telemetry, no analytics, no "phone home." Your machines are your business.
  • Free + open where it makes sense. Support the studio if you want to feed the badger; nothing essential locked behind a paywall.
  • Polished or it doesn't ship. No DOS-window installers. No "pip install" instructions for end users.
