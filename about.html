🦡 THE STUDIO

About Honeybadger Software

A one-person studio building polished tools for the gear you actually own — 3D printers, the car in your garage, the machines at your plant.

The honey badger doesn't care. It chews through firmware bugs vendors won't fix, walks past "this can't be done" forum threads, and ships the polished thing anyway.

Honeybadger Software exists because too many great machines come wrapped in software that fights you. A 3D printer that needs a Raspberry Pi and a firmware conversion before it'll tell you the bed temp. A car packed with sensors you can't see. A factory floor full of data trapped in PLCs. The hardware is fine — the experience is broken.

So this studio builds the missing layer: one-click installers and open hardware that make the gear do what it always should have. No terminals. No "pip install" for end users. No telemetry phoning home. Honest about what works and what doesn't — every product ships with a Known Issues page on day one.

It's one developer across a lot of disciplines, which is exactly why the polish bar stays high: if it's not good enough to put my own name behind, it doesn't ship.

WHAT WE STAND ON

Five principles, no exceptions.

  • One-click installers, no terminals. A user shouldn't need to know what Python is to use the software.
  • Honest about limits. Every product ships with a Known Issues page on day one.
  • No telemetry, no analytics, no "phone home." Your machines are your business.
  • Free + open where it makes sense. Support the studio if you want to feed the badger; nothing essential behind a paywall.
  • Polished or it doesn't ship. No DOS-window installers. No half-finished features pretending to be done.
